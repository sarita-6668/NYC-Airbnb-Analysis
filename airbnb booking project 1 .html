Since it started in 2008, Airbnb has changed how people travel by giving guests and hosts a platform to create unique and personalized travel experiences. Over the years, Airbnb has become a popular service known worldwide, connecting millions of people. The information collected from Airbnb’s listings provides a huge opportunity to understand and improve the platform. This data can help make the platform safer, guide better business decisions, understand how users and hosts behave, improve how the platform works, plan smarter marketing, and come up with new services. The dataset includes around 49,000 entries with 16 different types of information, combining numbers and categories. By exploring and analyzing this data, we can find answers to interesting questions, like: 1. What can we learn about hosts and locations? 2. How can we use this data to predict things like prices, reviews, and popular locations? 3. Who are the most active hosts, and what makes them successful? 4. Which types of rooms are most popular in the best neighborhoods? 1. Data Importing and Cleaning

Key Insights:

  1. Harlem, located in Manhattan, is the most popular neighborhood, with Private Rooms being the most preferred room type.
  2. Manhattan has the highest number of listings, followed by Brooklyn and Queens. Staten Island has the fewest listings.
  3. Manhattan and Brooklyn together account for 87% of the total listings in New York City.
  4. Brooklyn and Manhattan are the most popular neighborhoods for people looking for rentals.
  5. Queens has fewer listings than Manhattan, so efforts should be made to encourage more hosts to list their properties in Queens.
  6. The highest demand is for Private Rooms and Entire Homes/Apartments, with most people looking for more affordable rental options.

Conclusion:

From the data, we can see that Manhattan leads in both the number of listings and rental prices. This makes sense because Manhattan is famous for its attractions like museums, stores, parks, and theaters, drawing a lot of tourists, which pushes up rental prices.

Brooklyn is the second most popular neighborhood with a significant number of listings, but its rental prices are more affordable compared to Manhattan.

On the other hand, neighborhoods like Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island have fewer listings, especially on Staten Island. These areas are more residential, and people may choose them to save money or visit family and friends.

It would have been helpful to include additional data, such as positive and negative reviews, to further understand the popularity of each neighborhood.
